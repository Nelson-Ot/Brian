About Me.

Aspiring Research Scientist

I am currently pursuing a master’s degree in biotechnology at Griffith University, Australia, with just one more semester to go. Originally from Kenya, I hold a Bachelor of Pharmacy degree from Mount Kenya University. Before shifting my focus to research, I practiced as a pharmacist, where my passion for science and the well-being of others grew.

My decision to transition into biotechnology was driven by a desire to engage in research and contribute to meaningful scientific discoveries. This passion led me to travel to Australia, where I have made it my new home. Although far from home, I have made new friends from different cultures and backgrounds .Recently, I completed my placement in the microbiology department at SAS Laboratory. There, I deepened my expertise in water testing and microbiological techniques, this was a welcome move to get hands-on experience in a high-level laboratory setting.

Beyond academics, I am motivated by a deep sense of care for others. Back in Kenya, I spent some of my free time supporting blind children, an experience that reinforced my desire to make a positive impact on people’s lives

In my leisure, I enjoy watching movies where research scientists unravel complex scientific puzzles, which fuels my imagination and drive to be a part of groundbreaking solutions. I aspire to join a leading team of scientists working on global health challenges, with the ultimate dream of contributing to the discovery of a cure for cancer.
